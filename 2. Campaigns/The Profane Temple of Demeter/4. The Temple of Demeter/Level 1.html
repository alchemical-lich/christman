Level 1: The Ruined Temple

The ground floor of the Temple of Demeter lies open to the sky in places, its limestone walls cracked and stained by centuries of neglect. Thick vines choke the colonnade, and the stench of rot both ancient grain and fresh refuse hangs in the still air. Margos of the Maw and his raiders have claimed the Great Hall and surrounding chambers as their fortified base, but entire wings remain sealed or collapsed, untouched since the temple's fall.

Active Factions:

  • The Gulfak Marauders Margos of the Maw (Ogre) leads ~16 goblins, 6 orcs, and 2 war bears from the Great Hall deep in the northern section. He controls the central and northern portions of the level. The goblins are restless and poorly disciplined; several are looking for a chance to desert. Two orc lieutenants enforce Margos's will. A captured wizard, Sarikos, is imprisoned in their territory.
  • The Restless Dead Skeletons and zombies in the Shrine Wing, animated by demonic corruption seeping from below. They do not coordinate or leave their area but attack any living creature on sight.
  • Mother Galvikta The last priestess of Demeter maintains a secret hidden shrine (Room 29) accessible only through a concealed passage. She observes the other factions and may approach worthy adventurers.

Hooks:

  • Local farmers seek adventurers to stop the raids plaguing the Aklieos region.
  • A scholar in a nearby town seeks information about the Fountain of Demeter and will pay for rubbings of any inscriptions found.
  • Rumours of "cursed grain that drives men mad with hunger" have reached a merchant guild, who will pay to recover uncontaminated supplies.
  • A wizard named Hekatos (on Level 2) is desperate to recover his captured disciple Sarikos from Margos's prison.

Entry & Forecourt

Room 1: The Broken Gate

Dimensions: 10' x 30' passage, 15' ceiling
Purpose: Temple main entrance

The temple's main entrance is a pair of massive oak doors, one torn from its hinges and leaning against the exterior wall, the other hanging open at an angle. Deep gouges in the timber suggest something large forced its way through. Beyond, a flagstone passage runs 30 feet north to the courtyard. Dried blood stains the threshold, and the air carries the smell of goblin filth and old smoke from deeper inside.

Features:

  • Exits: Double doors south to exterior (broken open); open passage north to Room 2 (Courtyard)
  • Illumination: Daylight from entrance, fading to dim light further in
  • Goblin Sentries: Two Goblins and one Orc Overseer (HD 1, 7hp, battle axe) crouch behind the fallen door slab. The goblins gamble with knucklebones while the orc watches the approach with bored contempt. The goblins are inattentive surprised on 1-3 on d6 but the orc is only surprised on 1. If intruders are spotted, the orc blows a signal horn (alerts Room 6 in 1 round) while the goblins fire arrows.
  • Door Damage: The gouges on the doors are claw marks from something far larger than the current occupants possibly whatever creature previously laired here before Margos displaced it.
  • Wall Graffiti: Crude goblin script near the entrance reads "GULFAK" with an arrow pointing inside.

Investigation:

  • Examining the claw marks: Too large for an ogre four parallel gouges, 6 inches deep. Something with massive claws. No further trace of the creature.
  • Listening at the passage: Distant goblin chatter and clanking from Room 6 (successful listen check reveals approximate numbers "sounds like a lot").

Room 2: The Overgrown Courtyard

Dimensions: 60' x 60', open to sky
Purpose: Temple forecourt and central hub

A broad courtyard open to the sky. Cracked flagstones push up between clumps of yellowed grass and thorny scrub. A dry fountain basin stands at the centre its carved stone figure of Demeter has been defaced, the head knocked off and lying in the weeds nearby. A few inches of brackish, oily water collect in the basin, giving off a faint sour smell. Goblin refuse bones, broken crates, excrement litters the edges.

Features:

  • Exits: South to Room 1 (Gate); north to Room 6 (Antechamber) via passage; east to Room 16 (Granary Corridor) and Room 3 (Collapsed Portico) via rubble passage; west to Room 5 (Colonnade); northeast to Room 33 (Watchtower) via crumbling stair
  • Illumination: Full daylight (open to sky)
  • Fountain: Stone basin, 8 feet in diameter. Carved base depicts Demeter pouring water from a ewer. Head has been knocked off lies in weeds 10 feet away. Oily, discoloured water collects naturally.
  • Vegetation: Thorny scrub and yellowed grass. Thick vines climb the colonnade to the west.
  • Refuse: Goblin litter along north and east walls bones, cracked pottery, rotting food scraps.

Investigation:

  • Drinking or touching the fountain water: Save vs. Poison or suffer nausea (-1 to attack rolls, 1d4 turns). The water radiates faint corruption if Detect Magic is cast.
  • Casting Purify Food and Water on the fountain: A brief shimmer of golden light ripples across the basin reveals an inscription on the inner rim in archaic script: "She who nourishes demands remembrance below." This hints at the Inner Sanctum on the lower levels.
  • Examining Demeter's severed head: The stone face is serene despite the damage. A successful search (1-in-6) reveals faint traces of gold leaf in the carved eyes worth nothing, but confirms this was once a richly decorated shrine.
  • Searching the refuse: 3 turns of digging through goblin litter yields 1d10 cp, a bent iron spike, and a human finger bone (from a previous captive).

Room 3: The Collapsed Portico

Dimensions: 15' x 40' (irregular rubble slope)
Purpose: Former eastern colonnade, now alternate passage

The eastern portico has partially collapsed, creating a treacherous slope of broken limestone columns and shattered clay roof tiles. Dust motes swirl in the light filtering through gaps in the remaining ceiling. The rubble slopes upward to the east, terminating at a ragged gap in the wall that opens into the Granary Wing (Room 18).

Features:

  • Exits: West to Room 2 (Courtyard); east through rubble gap to Room 18 (Collapsed Store)
  • Illumination: Dim, filtered daylight through cracks in remaining ceiling
  • Rubble Slope: Climbing requires care. Dexterity check or 1-in-6 chance of a minor rockslide (1d4 damage, noise alerts Room 6).
  • Tripwire Alarm: A crude tripwire strung across the rubble by goblins at the halfway point, tied to a cluster of tin bells. 1-in-6 to detect (Thief abilities apply). If triggered, the jangling alerts goblins in Room 6 within 1 round.
  • Tracks: Goblin footprints in the dust, plus tiny rat tracks. The goblins use this as a shortcut occasionally.

Investigation:

  • Careful examination of the rubble: The columns are carved with wheat-sheaf motifs once part of the temple's grand eastern colonnade. The collapse appears structural (age and neglect), not deliberate.
  • Listening at the gap: Faint scratching sounds from Room 18 (rats), and the musty smell of ancient grain.

Room 4: The Gatehouse Alcove

Dimensions: 10' x 10', 10' ceiling
Purpose: Former guard post

A narrow alcove beside the main gate, once a guard post for temple sentries. A cracked stone bench lines the south wall, and an iron weapon rack now rusted bare hangs on the north wall. Someone has scratched into the stone in crude Common: "MARGOS TAKES WHAT MARGOS WANTS."

Features:

  • Exits: Open archway west to Room 1 (Gate passage)
  • Illumination: Dim (light from entrance spills in)
  • Stone Bench: Cracked but functional. The underside has been scratched with tally marks a bored goblin's count of days.
  • Weapon Rack: Empty, rusted. Three mounting brackets for spears, all empty.
  • Loose Flagstone: Beneath the bench, one flagstone sits slightly higher than its neighbours.

Investigation:

  • Examining the loose flagstone (1-in-6 to notice, automatic with Find Traps): A leather satchel wedged beneath. Contains 45 gp (old Hyperborean coin, stamped with wheat sheaves) and a tarnished silver holy symbol of Demeter (25 gp, or 50 gp to a collector or cleric of Demeter).
  • Reading the tally marks: 23 marks. Margos has been here approximately three weeks matches the background timeline.

Treasure (hidden): 45 gp, silver holy symbol of Demeter (25 gp).

Room 5: The Colonnade

Dimensions: 80' x 10' passage, 20' ceiling (partially collapsed)
Purpose: Western processional walkway

A long colonnade runs along the western wall of the temple, connecting the courtyard to the Shrine Wing. Thick limestone pillars carved with intertwining grape vines and wheat sheaves support what remains of a vaulted ceiling. Several pillars have collapsed, creating piles of rubble that narrow the passage. Dried vines and creeping ivy cling to every surface. The air is cooler here, shielded from the sun, and the silence is notable after the bustle of the courtyard.

Features:

  • Exits: East to Room 2 (Courtyard) at south end; west to Room 23 (Hall of Offerings) at north end; a narrow gap in the west wall halfway along leads to Room 24 (Altar Room)
  • Illumination: Dim, filtered light through gaps in the ceiling; darker at north end
  • Pillars: 8 pillars remaining (4 collapsed). The intact ones are carved with Demeter's sacred plants. One pillar near the midpoint has fresh scratch marks at its base claw marks, recent.
  • Rubble: Two collapsed pillars create partial barriers. Characters can climb over (1 round) or squeeze through gaps.
  • Sounds: The colonnade acts as an echo chamber. Sounds from the Shrine Wing carry faintly occasional scraping, a distant moan.

Investigation:

  • Examining the claw marks on the pillar: Similar to those on the main gate (Room 1) but smaller. Something has been using this passage recently. Tracks in the dust suggest a large quadruped possibly one of Margos's war bears, or something else.
  • The ceiling: Partially collapsed. A character who climbs a pillar can see over the walls into the courtyard (Room 2) and the roofline of the Shrine Wing. Useful for scouting.
  • Listening: A successful listen check from the north end picks up very faint chanting barely audible, coming from below (the demon's influence seeping through the floors).
  • Hollow Pillar (secret): The fourth pillar from the south sounds hollow when struck (1-in-6 to notice, dwarf 2-in-6). A section of the carved wheat-sheaf design rotates, revealing a narrow cavity inside the pillar. Contains a Wand of Magic Detection (7 charges) wrapped in oilcloth hidden here by a fleeing priest during the temple's fall. Also contains 120 gp in old Hyperborean gold coin and a rolled parchment with a prayer to Demeter (functions as a one-use Dispel Evil scroll if read by a cleric).

Margos's Stronghold

Room 6: The Antechamber

Dimensions: 30' x 20', 15' ceiling
Purpose: Former temple vestibule, now marauder guard post

A broad chamber that once served as the vestibule between the courtyard and the temple's inner halls. The floor is flagstone, scarred with drag marks and boot prints. Two crude wooden barricades have been erected from broken pews and overturned stone benches, creating a chokepoint. A brazier of burning oil casts flickering orange light and trails of black smoke. The goblins on duty here are more alert than the sentries at the gate Margos beats the ones who fall asleep.

Features:

  • Exits: South passage to Room 2 (Courtyard); north passage to Room 7 (Goblin Warren) and Room 10 (Great Hall); west passage to Room 8 (Kitchen)
  • Illumination: Firelight from brazier (bright in centre, dim at edges)
  • Barricades: Two waist-high barriers of broken furniture. Provide half cover (-2 AC bonus) to defenders behind them. Arranged to create a kill-zone at the southern entrance.
  • Tripwires: Two tripwires strung at ankle height across the southern approach. 1-in-6 to detect (Thief abilities apply). If triggered: clay pots filled with gravel crash to the floor no damage, but the racket alerts Room 7 and Room 10 in 1 round.
  • Goblin Guards: 4 Goblins and 1 Orc Sergeant (HD 2, 14hp, chainmail, battle axe) on duty. The goblins crouch two behind each barricade with short bows and 10 arrows each. The orc sergeant paces between the barricades, kicking any goblin who dozes off. He wears a necklace of severed ears his tally of kills.

Investigation:

  • Examining the barricades: Crude but effective. Made from broken temple pews carved wood, once fine. The goblins have hammered iron spikes into the tops (1d4 damage to anyone trying to vault over).
  • The brazier: Iron bowl on a stone pillar, filled with burning oil. Could be overturned (STR check) to create a fire hazard burning oil spreads 10' radius, 1d6 damage per round for 2 rounds.
  • Behind the barricades: A crude wooden shield with Margos's mark (a fist), a waterskin of sour beer, and a goblin's "lucky charm" a dried human ear on a string.

Room 7: The Goblin Warren

Dimensions: 25' x 30', 10' ceiling
Purpose: Former storage room, now goblin barracks

A chaotic mess of filthy bedrolls, gnawed bones, broken crates, and excrement. The low ceiling and lack of ventilation create a stifling, eye-watering atmosphere. Ten pallets are arranged in rough clusters the goblins sleep in shifts, four or five at a time. A constant background of bickering, snoring, and the scrape of crude blades being sharpened on stone.

Features:

  • Exits: Door south to Room 6 (Antechamber); passage east to Room 10 (Great Hall)
  • Illumination: Two sputtering tallow candles (dim)
  • Goblins: 8-10 Goblins present at any given time (remainder on patrol or guard duty). Eating, sleeping, gambling, or fighting over scraps. Morale is low many want to desert.
  • Bedrolls: Filthy pallets arranged in clusters. Goblins sleep in shifts.
  • Crates: Broken crates along the walls contain looted supplies stolen foodstuffs, crude weapons, bundles of cloth, a few clay pots of dubious stew.

Investigation:

  • Searching bedrolls (requires clearing the room first, 3 turns): Scattered coins and minor loot throughout (see Treasure below).
  • Talking to goblins (if captured/intimidated): They are terrified of Margos but also terrified of "the dead things in the west wing" and "the wizard prisoner who screams at night." They know the layout of Rooms 6-15 and can describe Margos's routine.
  • One goblin, Skrit, is an ambitious runt who secretly wants to overthrow his orc handlers. He'll approach the party if they spare goblins offers to betray Margos in exchange for safe passage and "all the shiny bits I can carry."

Room 33: The Watchtower Ruin

Dimensions: 20' x 20' base, 30' tall (partially collapsed)
Purpose: Former temple watchtower, now ruined observation point

Accessible from the courtyard (Room 2) via a crumbling external stair, this partially collapsed watchtower juts above the temple's roofline. The lower floor is open to the sky where the upper stories have fallen in. The remaining walls rise 30 feet, providing a commanding view of the temple complex, the surrounding countryside, and the river Lyos in the distance. The upper portion is precarious stone blocks are loose and the floor creaks ominously.

Features:

  • Exits: Crumbling stair south to Room 2 (Courtyard); collapsed section of roof east (leads to Granary Wing roof, accessible with rope/climbing drop down into Room 21's grain chute area)
  • Illumination: Full daylight (exterior)
  • Vantage Point: From the watchtower, a character can observe:
    • The courtyard layout (Room 2) and the general shape of the temple wings
    • Smoke rising from Margos's camp (Rooms 6-15 area) indicates occupation
    • The Granary Wing roof partially collapsed, with a gap that could serve as alternate entry
    • Movement in the courtyard or along the colonnade (Room 5) useful for scouting patrols
    • The road south toward Peloros and the river Lyos
  • Precarious Upper Floor: The upper section is unstable. A character weighing more than 150 lbs who enters the upper floor must save vs. Paralysis or a section collapses (2d6 falling damage, crash audible from Rooms 1, 2, and 6 alerts goblins).
  • Goblin Lookout: 50% chance a Goblin is posted here as lookout (Margos's orders, but the goblin is lazy asleep 75% of the time, looking the wrong way the rest).

Investigation:

  • Scouting from the tower: Provides tactical advantage the party can estimate goblin patrol patterns (Room 6), count smoke plumes, and identify the alternate entry to the Granary Wing. This information grants +1 to surprise rolls for the party's next encounter in the temple (they were forewarned by observation).
  • The collapsed roof route: A 15-foot gap between the watchtower and the Granary Wing roof. A rope and grappling hook can bridge it. Landing on the Granary roof, a character can descend through a hole into Room 21 (the Millroom) via the grain chute shaft. This bypasses Rooms 1, 2, 6, and 16 entirely.
  • Examining the tower's construction: The stonework is identical to the rest of the temple. Carved inscriptions on the interior walls record weather observations and harvest dates centuries of data, useless but atmospheric.
