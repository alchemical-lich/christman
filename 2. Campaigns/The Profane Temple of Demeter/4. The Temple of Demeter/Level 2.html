Level 2: The Vaults Below

Beneath the ruined temple, the air changes. The crumbling limestone gives way to dressed stone blocks --- older, harder, built to last centuries. The corridors are narrower and lower (8-foot ceilings unless noted), and the ever-present smell of rotting grain from above is replaced by damp stone, alchemical residue, and something sour and organic that grows stronger toward the granary silos. Faded frescoes line the walls, depicting the temple's underground functions: grain storage, ritual preparation, priestly quarters, and the ceremonial route to the Inner Sanctum below.

Hekatos the Learned and his surviving disciples have established a fortified research camp in the eastern chambers, methodically studying the ward-stone on this level and searching for the others that maintain the seal imprisoning demons below. The western half of the level is dominated by the ancient grain silos --- cavernous vertical shafts that span both Level 2 and Level 3B --- where the Worm Illaktamus has made its lair.

Active Factions:

  • The Circle of Hekatos --- Hekatos the Learned (Magician 7) and 3 remaining disciples occupy the eastern chambers (Rooms 8--15). They are methodical, cautious, and well-warded. Hekatos is obsessed with destroying the ward-stones and reaching the Fountain of Demeter. He has located one ward-stone (Room 12) and suspects the others are on the deeper levels. He is completely unaware that demons wait below the seal.
  • The Worm Illaktamus --- Illaktamus, the Grain-Devourer (HD 10, 60hp) lairs in the western grain silos (Rooms 18--22). A colossal predator operating on pure animal instinct. Vulnerable to fire. The silos extend downward into Level 3B.
  • The Restless Dead --- Undead priests and temple guardians haunt the ceremonial chambers (Rooms 4--7), animated by the demon's corruption seeping upward through the stone.

Hooks:

  • Hekatos will pay 500 gp and a magic item for the rescue of Sarikos from Level 1.
  • One of the three ward-stones is on this level (Room 12). Mother Galvikta wants it protected; Hekatos wants it destroyed. The other two are on deeper levels.
  • Theron's spellbook is lost somewhere in the grain silos --- Hekatos will pay 800 gp for its return.
  • The grain silos connect downward to Level 3B, offering a dangerous but direct route to the deeper levels.

Arrival and Processional

Room 1: The Stair Landing

Dimensions: 20' x 20', 10' ceiling
Purpose: Arrival point from Level 1 (Room 30, Grand Staircase)

The wide staircase from Level 1 deposits the party in a square landing chamber. The walls are dressed limestone, noticeably better constructed than the crumbling ruins above. A faded mosaic on the floor depicts Demeter holding a cornucopia, now cracked down the middle. Two passages lead from the landing: east (a broad corridor) and south (a narrower passage). The air is cooler and damp. A faint dripping echoes from somewhere south.

Features:

  • Exits: Up (stairs to Level 1 Room 30); east to Room 3 (Processional Corridor); south to Room 16 (Silo Approach)
  • Illumination: Dark (no light source)
  • Mosaic: Demeter's cornucopia. A cleric who examines it (1 turn) notices the crack follows the line of an old chisel mark --- someone deliberately damaged this mosaic long ago.
  • Defensive Position: Hekatos considered establishing a guard post here but decided it was too exposed. Scratch marks on the east wall are his disciples' navigational marks (arrow pointing east, in chalk). A character who recognises these as wizard's marks (INT check or Detect Magic) understands someone has been through here regularly.

Investigation:

  • The chalk marks: Fresh --- no more than a few weeks old. The arrows always point east, toward Hekatos's territory.
  • The mosaic crack: The chisel damage is old (centuries). It was defaced during or after the temple's fall.
  • Listening south: A faint, rhythmic grinding sound from deep below --- Illaktamus shifting in the silos, barely audible.

Treasure: None.

Room 2: The Collapsed Shaft

Dimensions: Irregular cavern, roughly 20' across, 15' ceiling (open shaft above)
Purpose: Connection from Level 1 (Room 31, Collapsed Passage)

A rough natural cavern formed by structural collapse. The ceiling is an open shaft rising 15 feet to Level 1 (Room 31, in the Spoiled Grain Store above). Rubble and dirt cover the floor. Rat droppings are everywhere, and the smell of rotting grain drifts down from above. A low tunnel (4 feet high, requiring crouching) leads south to Room 16. A wider passage leads east to Room 3.

Features:

  • Exits: Up 15' (shaft to Level 1 Room 31, requires rope or climbing); east to Room 3 (Processional Corridor); south crawlway to Room 16 (Silo Approach, 4' high, 30' long)
  • Illumination: Dim light from above (if Level 1 is lit)
  • Unstable: The shaft edges are crumbly. Moving within 5' of the shaft without care requires a save vs. Paralysis or 1d6 debris falls from above (on the character, or on anyone climbing).
  • Rat Nest: A colony of normal rats nests in the rubble. Not dangerous, but their squeaking alerts anything nearby.

Investigation:

  • The south crawlway: 4 feet high and 4 feet wide --- all characters must crawl. Quarter speed in plate armour, half speed in chain. Combat in the crawlway is extremely difficult: no room to swing two-handed weapons, -2 to attack rolls with one-handed weapons, no shield benefit. Spells with somatic components require a save vs. Spells to cast successfully. If a tremor occurs (random encounter 9) while characters are in the crawlway, save vs. Paralysis or take 2d6 damage from collapsing stone. Leads to the silo approach, bypassing the Processional Corridor.
  • Signs of use: Faint boot prints in the dirt --- someone has been through here. These are old tracks from one of Hekatos's disciples scouting this area weeks ago.

Treasure: 8 sp and a rusted dagger (mundane) among the rubble --- dropped by a goblin who fell from above.

Room 3: The Processional Corridor

Dimensions: 80' long, 10' wide, 10' ceiling
Purpose: Ceremonial route connecting the stair landing to the inner chambers

A long, straight corridor running east--west, its walls covered in elaborate frescoes depicting the annual Harvest Procession. The paintings show robed priests carrying sheaves of grain, amphorae of wine, and cages of doves toward a great hall. The procession terminates at the eastern end with a grand depiction of the Inner Sanctum --- the Fountain of Demeter surrounded by golden light. The frescoes grow progressively darker toward the east: the sky in the paintings shifts from bright blue to deep purple, the priests' expressions shift from joy to solemnity, and the final panel shows the priests descending into shadow.

Features:

  • Exits: West to Room 1 (Stair Landing) and Room 2 (Collapsed Shaft); east to Room 4 (Robing Hall); south doors at the midpoint to Room 6 (Hall of Inscriptions)
  • Illumination: Dark. However, the frescoes have been treated with a mineral pigment that faintly reflects torchlight, creating an eerie effect as the procession seems to move in flickering light.
  • Pressure Plate Trap (midpoint): A 5' section of floor is a pressure plate. Stepping on it triggers a spray of corroded needles from the left wall (2d4 damage, save vs. Poison or contract a wasting disease: -1 CON per day until cured by Cure Disease). Detect traps: 2-in-6 (Thief abilities apply). The needles were originally intended as a security measure against tomb robbers.
  • Hekatos's Ward: Near the east end, a thin chalk line on the floor marks one of Hekatos's magical alarm perimeters. Crossing it triggers a silent alarm audible only to Hekatos (see Room 10). Detect Magic reveals a faint abjuration aura on the chalk line. The line can be disrupted by scuffing it (but Hekatos will notice it's been tampered with on his next patrol).

Investigation:

  • Studying the frescoes (2 turns): Reveals the processional route through the temple --- from this corridor through the Robing Hall (Room 4), past the Purification Baths (Room 5), and down to the lower levels. This information is valuable for navigation.
  • The final panel (Inner Sanctum): Shows the Fountain as a stone basin filled with golden water, surrounded by kneeling priests. A character who studies it with Detect Magic sees a faint residual aura --- the painting itself was blessed, though the blessing has mostly faded.
  • The pressure plate: The mechanism is ancient and corroded. A Thief can disable it with a standard Find/Remove Traps check. Alternatively, the plate is visible as a slightly raised section of floor if specifically examined.

Treasure: None (the frescoes are immovable and fragile).

The Ceremonial Chambers

Room 4: The Robing Hall

Dimensions: 40' x 30', 10' ceiling
Purpose: Where priests donned ceremonial vestments before rituals

A wide chamber with stone wardrobes lining three walls, most smashed or rotted open. Fragments of embroidered fabric --- gold thread on white linen, now grey and brittle --- litter the floor. A large bronze mirror (5 feet tall, tarnished green) stands against the north wall, its surface too corroded to reflect clearly. The room smells of mildew and old cloth.

Features:

  • Exits: West to Room 3 (Processional Corridor); east to Room 5 (Purification Baths); north door to Room 7 (Vigil Chamber); south passage to Room 8 (Outer Ward)
  • Illumination: Dark
  • Bronze Mirror: Tarnished but intact. Worth 50 gp if polished and transported (heavy --- 200 lbs). If polished in place (2 turns of work), the mirror reveals a faint image behind the viewer --- a ghostly priest adjusting his vestments. The image is a harmless residual haunting and vanishes after 1 round.
  • Undead Encounter: 3 Ghouls lurk behind the smashed wardrobes, lying still among the fabric scraps until a living creature enters the room. They attack with surprise (1--3 on d6) unless the party is specifically checking behind furniture.

  • Origin: These were once temple acolytes. Their vestment fragments are still visible beneath the filth.

  • Searching the wardrobes (after combat): Most contain only rags. One wardrobe at the far end has a false bottom (1-in-6 to detect, dwarf 2-in-6). Inside: a ceremonial stole of gold-threaded linen, folded and wrapped in waxed cloth. Worth 75 gp, or 200 gp to a collector or temple.
  • The bronze mirror: A character who looks into the polished mirror while holding a holy symbol sees the room as it once was --- clean, well-lit, full of priests preparing for a ceremony. This vision lasts 1 round and reveals the locations of all exits, including the south passage to Room 8 (which is partially concealed by collapsed masonry).

Treasure: Ceremonial stole (75 gp / 200 gp to collector). Ghouls carry nothing.

Room 5: The Purification Baths

Dimensions: 30' x 30', 8' ceiling
Purpose: Ritual bathing chambers for priests before ceremonies

Three stone basins (each 8' x 4', 3' deep) are sunk into the floor, connected by clay pipes to a water source that has long since dried up. The basins are empty and cracked, but a thick coating of greenish-black mould covers the interior of all three. The room is humid despite the dry basins --- moisture beads on the stone walls. The mould releases a faintly sweet, cloying smell.

Features:

  • Exits: West to Room 4 (Robing Hall); east to Room 6 (Hall of Inscriptions)
  • Illumination: Dark
  • Toxic Mould: The mould is a corrupted form of a once-beneficial fungus that kept the baths clean. Now it releases spores when disturbed. Any character who enters a basin or disturbs the mould (including combat in the room) releases a cloud of spores in a 10' radius. Save vs. Poison or suffer 1d4 damage and -1 to all rolls for 1 hour (persistent coughing and watery eyes). A cloth over the mouth grants +2 to the save. Fire destroys the mould in a basin (1 round per basin), but the smoke is also toxic (same save, -2 penalty).
  • Pipe System: The clay pipes (approximately 2 feet in diameter) run under the floor and connect to the Flooded Cistern (Room 24). A Small-sized character (halfling, gnome) or any character magically reduced to comparable size could crawl through the pipes, but they are partially collapsed and require 3 turns to navigate. Medium-sized characters cannot fit. This provides a secret route to Room 24.

Investigation:

  • Examining the pipes: Lead under the floor. A character who puts their ear to a pipe hears distant dripping --- the Cistern (Room 24) still holds water.
  • Cleaning a basin (2 turns, requires scraping): Beneath the mould, the basin floor is inscribed with prayers to Demeter. A cleric who reads them aloud gains the benefit of a Bless spell for 1 hour (one-time effect, does not repeat).
  • The mould itself: An alchemist or herbalist can identify it as Demeter's Veil, a fungus that was once used to purify water. Its corruption mirrors the temple's --- if the Fountain were restored, this mould would revert to its beneficial form.

Treasure: None.

Room 6: The Hall of Inscriptions

Dimensions: 50' x 20', 12' ceiling (unusually tall)
Purpose: Record-keeping and lore chamber

The walls of this chamber are covered floor-to-ceiling in carved text --- thousands of lines of archaic Hyperborean script recording the temple's history, its rules, and its sacred rites. The text is small and dense, requiring close examination to read. Three stone reading lecterns stand in a row down the centre of the room, one of which has been recently cleared of dust and has fresh chalk marks beside it --- Hekatos has been here.

Features:

  • Exits: West to Room 5 (Purification Baths); north to Room 3 (Processional Corridor, via door at corridor midpoint); east passage to Room 12 (The Ward-Stone Chamber); south to Room 23 (Sealed Passage)
  • Illumination: Dark
  • Inscriptions: Contain the full history of the temple and its priesthood. A character who reads archaic Hyperborean (or uses Comprehend Languages) can learn:
    • The locations and purpose of the three ward-stones (1 turn of study)
    • The nature of the seal binding the demons (2 turns)
    • The proper ritual for cleansing the Fountain of Demeter (3 turns)
    • The existence and purpose of the emergency passage to Level 3A (1 turn)
  • Hekatos's Notes: The chalk marks beside the lectern are Hekatos's annotations. He has translated key passages about the ward-stones. His notes reveal he has identified the ward-stone in Room 12 and is searching for the other two, which the inscriptions suggest are on deeper levels. A character who reads these notes understands Hekatos's plan --- and could warn him about the demons, or could race to protect the ward-stones.

Investigation:

  • Reading the ward-stone passages: The text describes three "anchors of binding" placed at sacred points within the temple. One "in the eastern vault beneath the processional" (Room 12), one "among the tombs of the high dead" (Level 3A Room 9), and one "at the ancient altar beneath the waters" (Level 3B Room 9). The text warns that destroying any anchor weakens the seal proportionally.
  • Hekatos's annotations: Careful, methodical. He has circled the ward-stone passages and written "MUST DESTROY" beside them. Red chalk marks confirm Room 12, with question marks beside the references to the deeper two. He has also noted: "Adrianna --- high priestess? Sealed voluntarily? Or trapped?" This suggests he is beginning to question the official history, but has drawn the wrong conclusions.
  • The third lectern: Holds a crumbling scroll that disintegrates if handled roughly. If carefully extracted (roll equal to or under DEX score on 1d20, or Thief pick-pockets ability at standard percentage), it contains a Scroll of Protection from Evil. Failure destroys the scroll irreparably.

Treasure: Scroll of Protection from Evil (fragile, requires careful handling). Hekatos's research notes (valuable to Galvikta or other scholars, worth 100 gp to an academy).

Room 7: The Vigil Chamber

Dimensions: 20' diameter circular room, 10' domed ceiling
Purpose: Meditation and overnight vigils before major ceremonies

A perfectly circular room with a domed ceiling painted to resemble the night sky --- silver stars on deep blue. A single stone bench rings the wall, and a low stone altar stands in the centre, bare and cold. The air is unnaturally still. Candle stubs in bronze holders ring the altar, their wax pooled and solidified centuries ago.

Warning Signs: The door to this room is carved with a Hyperborean inscription: "None may break the vigil who do not carry Demeter's light." A cleric who reads the inscription senses a powerful undead presence within. The painted stars on the dome are visible through the open doorway and pulse faintly --- any character with Detect Magic active sees a strong necromantic aura filling the room before entering.

Features:

  • Exits: South door to Room 4 (Robing Hall)
  • Illumination: Dark. The painted stars on the dome faintly glimmer when magical light sources are present (reacting to magical energy).
  • The Wraith: A Wraith haunts this chamber --- the spirit of Archon Phaelos, a priest who was meditating here when Adrianna profaned the Inner Sanctum. The shock of the desecration killed him, and his spirit has been trapped ever since, bound by grief and rage. The wraith manifests 1 round after any living creature enters, rising from the altar as a column of dark smoke that coalesces into a robed figure with hollow, burning eyes.

Wraith (Archon Phaelos)

Armor Class 3 [16]
Hit Dice 5+3 (28hp)
Attacks 1 x touch (1d6 + energy drain)
THAC0 15 [+4]
Movement 120' (40'), fly 240' (80')
Saving Throws D10 W11 P12 B13 S14 (F5)
Morale 12 (bound to this room, cannot leave)
Alignment Lawful Evil (corrupted by demonic influence)
XP 540
Special Energy Drain: Successful hit drains one level. Immune to Sleep, Charm, Hold, Cold, and non-magical weapons. Silver weapons deal half damage.
  • Tactics: The wraith manifests at the altar and speaks its warning (see below) before becoming hostile. It has a 1-round grace period during which it does not attack --- the party may act, speak, or retreat freely during this round. After this round, if no prayer or peaceful response is given, the wraith moves to block the exit and attacks the cleric first (if present). Cannot leave this room --- bound to the vigil it never completed. If turned, it dissipates into the altar for 2d6 turns before reforming.
  • Energy Drain: The wraith's energy drain is temporary --- drained levels return after 1d6 days of rest, representing spiritual exhaustion rather than permanent soul damage. Phaelos is not a predator; he is a grief-maddened guardian. (If the party lays him to rest, all drained levels are immediately restored.)
  • Destruction: If a cleric casts Bless on the altar and speaks a prayer to Demeter (the correct prayer is inscribed in Room 6, or Galvikta knows it), Phaelos's spirit is released. The wraith dissolves with a sigh of relief, and the altar glows golden for 1 turn. The candle stubs reignite with pale golden flames that burn for 24 hours. While the candles burn, the room functions as Protection from Evil for anyone resting inside. This room becomes a safe rest point --- parties who lay Phaelos to rest can camp here safely (no random encounters while the candles burn).
  • Communication: Before attacking, the wraith speaks one sentence in archaic Hyperborean: "The seal must hold. The seal must hold. THE SEAL MUST HOLD." It repeats this endlessly, growing louder. A character who responds with a prayer to Demeter (even improvised) causes the wraith to hesitate for 1 round (no attack), during which it can be spoken to. It can answer three questions about the temple's history before grief overwhelms it and it attacks.

Investigation:

  • The altar: A Detect Magic reveals faint necromantic and abjuration auras (the wraith's binding and the altar's faded holiness).
  • The dome: The star pattern is accurate --- it depicts the night sky as it appeared on the date of the temple's founding. An astronomer or sage could use this to determine the temple's age (approximately 500 years).
  • If Phaelos is laid to rest: The altar becomes a place of power. A cleric who prays here for 1 turn recovers one expended spell slot (1st or 2nd level only, once per day).

Treasure: If the wraith is destroyed (not just turned): the stone bench conceals a hidden compartment (1-in-6 to find, revealed automatically if Phaelos is laid to rest peacefully). Contains 350 gp in ancient gold, a silver holy symbol of Demeter (100 gp), and a Potion of Extra-Healing (3d6+3 hp).

Hekatos's Territory

Room 8: The Outer Ward

Dimensions: 30' x 20', 8' ceiling
Purpose: Hekatos's alarm perimeter and entry point

A plain stone chamber that once served as a staging area for temple guards. Hekatos has repurposed it as the outer edge of his territory. A chalk circle inscribed on the floor emits a faint magical hum --- an Alarm spell that alerts Hekatos when crossed. The room is clean compared to the rest of the level; the disciples sweep it regularly as part of their duties.

Features:

Room 19: The Upper Silo (East)

Purpose: Secondary grain storage silo

A smaller silo shaft, 30 feet in diameter. The grain level is higher here (only 10 feet below the loading arch from Room 17) because Illaktamus feeds primarily from the west silo. The grain surface is more stable but disturbed --- the worm passes through occasionally, leaving furrows and acidic streaks.

Features:

  • Exits: East arch to Room 17 (Loading Hall, 10' above grain --- easier access than Room 18); above to Room 20 (Silo Catwalks); below --- shaft descends 30' to Level 3B
  • Illumination: Dark
  • Illaktamus: The worm is here 30% of the time (when not in Room 18). It uses this silo as a secondary lair and a route between levels.
  • Victims' Remains: The eastern wall of the silo has a stone ledge (3 feet wide, 5 feet below the arch from Room 17) where several victims came to rest before the worm reached them. On the ledge:
    • A desiccated corpse in scholar's robes (one of Hekatos's earlier scouts, not Theron). Still wears a Cloak of Elvenkind (intact, functional).
    • A skeleton in chainmail clutching a torch stub and a sack containing 200 gp.
    • A leather map case containing a partial map of Level 3B (hand-drawn by the scout before death).

Investigation:

  • The stone ledge: Accessible from the loading arch by climbing down 5' (easy with rope). The ledge is stable and out of the grain, making it a relatively safe vantage point.
  • The scout's body: A disciple named Koros who disappeared 6 weeks ago. Hekatos assumes he was killed by the worm, but Koros actually died of a worm-acid wound infection while hiding on the ledge. His journal describes the silo layout, Illaktamus's habits, and the discovery that the shafts extend to a lower level.
  • The partial Level 3B map: Shows cavern passages connecting to the silo bottoms, a subterranean lake, and a passage marked "TO THE SEALED DOOR" with an arrow.

Treasure: Cloak of Elvenkind, 200 gp, Koros's journal (intelligence on Illaktamus and Level 3B), partial Level 3B map.

Room 20: The Silo Catwalks

Dimensions: Iron walkways, 3' wide, spanning both silo shafts at a height of 30' above the grain surface
Purpose: Maintenance access and grain-level monitoring

A network of narrow iron catwalks bolted to the silo walls, connecting the two shafts and providing access to grain-level markers, ventilation shafts, and inspection hatches. The catwalks are rusted but structurally sound (mostly). They offer a safe vantage point above Illaktamus's reach.

Features:

  • Exits: Iron ladder down to Room 17 (Loading Hall); catwalks cross over Rooms 18 and 19 (both silos); ventilation shaft up (too narrow for humanoids, but provides fresh air)
  • Illumination: Dark
  • Structural Integrity: The catwalks are generally sound, but three sections (marked with DM discretion) are weakened. A character in heavy armour crossing a weakened section has a 2-in-6 chance of the catwalk buckling (DEX save or fall 30' into the grain --- 3d6 damage, and you are now in the silo with Illaktamus).
  • Grain-Level Markers: Bronze plates on the wall showing grain levels in Hyperborean measurements. By comparing current levels to the markers, a character can determine that approximately 40% of the grain has been consumed.
  • Ventilation Shaft: Too narrow for a human (6 inches diameter) but provides a chimney effect that carries sound upward. Speaking into the shaft at normal volume can be heard (faintly) on Level 1 in the granary area.

Investigation:

  • Observation from above: The catwalks offer a perfect view of the grain surface below. Characters can see the furrows and disturbances that mark Illaktamus's passage. If Illaktamus is present, its movement is visible as a wave in the grain surface --- like watching a shark's fin in water.
  • The grain markers: Indicate original silo capacity. The grain stores were enormous --- enough to feed the temple and the town of Demestos for a full year.
  • Dropping objects: Objects dropped into the grain from the catwalks attract Illaktamus (if present) within 1d4 rounds. This can be used to test whether the worm is in a particular silo without risking entry.

Treasure: None on the catwalks themselves. However, a bronze maintenance hatch on the west catwalk opens to a small storage niche containing a coil of silk rope (50', lighter than standard rope) and a Potion of Climbing.

Room 21: The Grain-Master's Vault

Dimensions: 15' x 15', 8' ceiling
Purpose: The Grain-Master's secure office and records room

A small, sealed room behind a locked iron door (from Room 17). The room was designed to be airtight to protect valuable records, and it has succeeded --- the air inside is stale but dry, and the contents are remarkably well preserved. A heavy desk, a chair, and a wall of cubbyholes containing rolled scrolls and clay tablets fill the room.

Features:

  • Exits: North to Room 17 (Loading Hall, via locked iron door)
  • Illumination: Dark (sealed room, no light has entered in centuries)
  • Records: Detailed inventories of the temple's grain stores, trade records, and correspondence with regional merchants. Historically interesting but not immediately useful --- except for one document.
  • The Grain-Master's Log: A leather-bound journal describing the final weeks before the temple's fall. The Grain-Master (a functionary, not a priest) recorded everything: the strange behaviour of Adrianna, the growing sickness among the townspeople, the spoiled holy water, and --- critically --- the first sighting of "something in the grain." The final entry: "It came from below. I saw it in Silo Three. A worm of impossible size. The priests say it is a guardian awakened. I say it is an abomination. I have sealed the office and pray to Demeter for deliverance."
  • Treasure Coffer: An iron strongbox under the desk containing the Grain-Master's emergency funds: 600 gp, a gold signet ring (75 gp --- bearing the seal of the Temple of Demeter, recognisable by Galvikta), a set of merchant's scales (25 gp), and a Necklace of Adaptation (allows the wearer to breathe normally in any environment --- smoke, water, thin air, toxic spores. The Grain-Master kept this as insurance against silo fires and grain-dust explosions).
  • Galvikta's Cache: A small linen pouch tucked behind a loose stone in the wall (2-in-6 to find, or automatic if Galvikta directed the party here). Contains a Potion of Healing (2d4 hp), a sprig of dried wheat tied with golden thread, and a folded note in a woman's handwriting: "For whoever comes after me. The goddess watches. --- G." This is evidence that Galvikta has visited this room. The wheat sprig radiates faint divine magic and functions as a one-use Purify Food and Water if crushed into contaminated liquid.

Treasure: 600 gp, gold signet ring (75 gp), merchant's scales (25 gp), Necklace of Adaptation, Galvikta's cache (Potion of Healing, blessed wheat sprig), Grain-Master's Log (valuable intelligence, confirms Illaktamus's origins).

Room 22: The Silo Floor Access

Dimensions: 20' x 20', with open shafts descending 30' to Level 3B
Purpose: Lower maintenance access for the grain silos

At the base of the silo shafts, below the main grain level, a maintenance chamber connects the bottoms of both silos to a passage leading to Level 3B. This room is partially buried in grain that has trickled down from above. The grain here is the oldest in the temple --- slightly fermented, with a sour, alcoholic smell. Iron rungs set into the silo walls provide handholds for climbing, but many are rusted through. A heavy iron winch mechanism is bolted to the south wall --- the remains of a grain elevator.

Features:

  • Exits: Up through Silo West (Room 18, 30' climb through grain) and Silo East (Room 19, 30' climb through grain); south passage to Room 25 (Bone Stair to Level 3B)
  • Illumination: Dark
  • Grain Depth: 5--8 feet of loose grain covers the floor, making movement difficult (half speed). The grain is unstable near the silo shaft openings --- 2-in-6 chance of a grain avalanche (1d6 crushing damage, save vs. Paralysis or buried and suffocating).
  • Iron Rungs: Climbing the silo walls via the rungs: 70% of rungs are functional. Every 10 feet of climbing, a 1-in-6 chance a rung breaks (Dex save or fall, distance varies).
  • Illaktamus Access: The worm can reach this room from either silo shaft. It moves through the grain freely and is at home here. If the party enters from below (Level 3B), Illaktamus may be encountered descending through the grain.
  • Grain Elevator: The iron winch mechanism is corroded but repairable. A character with engineering knowledge or a dwarf can assess it in 1 turn: the chain is broken (but the 100' chain from Room 17 fits the mechanism). If repaired (2 turns of work, requires the chain and a successful INT check or dwarf stonecraft), the elevator can raise or lower one character safely between this room and Room 17 above --- bypassing the dangerous grain entirely. However, operating the winch is extremely loud (automatic alert to Illaktamus if within 2 rooms).
  • Worm Eggs (2): Partially buried in the grain near the west silo shaft, two leathery grey eggs (each 1 foot in diameter) sit in a shallow depression of acidic slime. These are Illaktamus's spawn --- immature and months from hatching, but warm to the touch. An alchemist or sage would pay 300 gp each for an intact egg. Destroying the eggs is easy (AC 9, 1 hp each) but the slime deals 1d4 acid damage to bare hands. If the party takes the eggs and Illaktamus later encounters their scent on the characters, it pursues relentlessly beyond its normal territory (increase morale to 12).

Investigation:

  • The south passage: A stone corridor, 10' wide, slopes gradually downward toward Room 25 and ultimately Level 3B. The air grows colder. The grain drifts thin and disappear within 20 feet of the passage opening.
  • The silo shaft walls: Inscribed with grain-level measurements and maintenance logs. One inscription reads: "Silo 3 condemned creature within. Do not unseal. Grain-Master Pylos, Year of the Red Harvest." This is the oldest reference to Illaktamus found in the dungeon.
  • Galvikta's ward: A small symbol of Demeter is scratched into the stone above the south passage entrance --- barely visible beneath a layer of grain dust. A cleric who touches it feels a brief pulse of warmth. Galvikta marked this as the safest route to Level 3B.

Treasure: 2 worm eggs (300 gp each to an alchemist or sage, dangerous to transport).

Connecting Passages

Room 23: The Sealed Passage

Dimensions: 10' wide, 30' long, 8' ceiling, blocked by a stone wall
Purpose: Former connection between Level 2 and Level 3A

A short corridor ending in a massive stone wall that has been deliberately constructed to block passage. The wall is 3 feet thick, made of fitted granite blocks, and sealed with mortar mixed with silver dust (a precaution against undead passing through). Carved into the facing surface is a single word in Hyperborean: SEALED.

Features:

  • Exits: East to Room 6 (Hall of Inscriptions)
  • Illumination: Dark
  • The Wall: Constructed during the temple's fall to block this route to the lower levels. Breaking through requires combined STR of 30+ (2 turns of work), a Knock spell (opens a 3' gap for 1 turn), or prolonged mining (4 hours with proper tools). Hekatos has examined the wall and decided it is not worth breaking through --- the spiral stairs (Level 1 Room 32) provide a better route to Level 3A.
  • Silver Mortar: The silver-laced mortar prevents incorporeal undead from passing through. If the wall is breached, this protection is lost and undead from Level 3A may begin appearing on Level 2 (increase random encounter frequency by 1-in-6).
  • The Offering Niche: Set into the east wall, a shallow niche (1' x 1') contains three empty stone bowls carved in the shapes of a sheaf of wheat, a cluster of grapes, and a dove. An inscription above the niche (in Hyperborean) reads: "The Goddess accepts three gifts before she opens the way." This is a puzzle mechanism --- placing the correct offerings in the bowls (grain or bread, wine, and a white feather or small bird) causes a hidden panel beside the sealed wall to slide open, revealing a narrow passage that bypasses the sealed wall entirely and descends to Level 3A. The correct offerings are depicted in the processional frescoes in Room 3 (the priests carry sheaves of grain, amphorae of wine, and cages of doves). All three offerings must be placed simultaneously. Incorrect offerings cause the bowls to tip and spill their contents (no harm, but the mechanism resets).

Investigation:

  • The word SEALED: Carved with deliberate, careful strokes --- this was not done in panic. The priests planned this blockage.
  • Detect Magic: The silver mortar radiates faint abjuration magic. The offering niche radiates faint transmutation magic.
  • Listening through the wall: Faint sounds from beyond --- dripping water, occasional scraping. Something is alive (or undead) on the other side.
  • The hidden panel: The bypass passage is 3' wide and descends steeply via a narrow spiral stair to Level 3A. It was the priests' emergency escape route. Unlike breaching the main wall, using this passage does not break the silver-mortar undead ward.

Treasure: None.

Room 24: The Flooded Cistern

Dimensions: 30' x 30', 12' ceiling, water depth 6'
Purpose: Temple water storage and purification

A large chamber that once stored and purified water for the temple's daily needs. The water source (connected to the Fountain of Demeter via underground channels) has been corrupted, and the cistern is now filled with 6 feet of dark, foul-smelling water. The water is opaque and has a faintly oily sheen. Ripples occasionally cross the surface without visible cause.

Features:

  • Exits: North to Room 13 (Archives); pipe access from Room 5 (Purification Baths, small characters only); submerged passage (see below)
  • Illumination: Dark. Torchlight reflects off the water surface but does not penetrate.
  • Corrupted Water: Contact with the water requires a save vs. Poison or suffer 1d4 damage (burning sensation) and -1 to STR for 1d6 hours. The corruption is demonic in origin --- Purify Food and Water cleanses a 5' cube (enough for one person to pass through safely). Bless cast on the water causes it to steam violently for 1 round (harmless) and purifies a 10' cube.
  • Submerged Passage: Below the water line on the west wall, a 5' wide submerged passage extends approximately 15' to a pocket of air (with a bronze handhold in the ceiling), then continues another 15' before emerging in the east wall of the underground lake on Level 3B, Room 6 (The Lakeshore Cavern), via Room 7 (The Drowned Passage). Total passage length is approximately 35'. Navigating each submerged leg requires holding breath for approximately 2 rounds. A mild current flows westward (toward the lake), making the descent easier than the return. Water Breathing or the Necklace of Adaptation (from Room 21) negates the breath-holding requirement.
  • Undead Hazard: A Lacedonian (aquatic ghoul, an advanced specimen empowered by the demon's corruption seeping through the cistern water) lurks beneath the surface. It attacks anyone who enters the water.

Room 25: The Bone Stair

Dimensions: Staircase, 10' wide, descending 40' in a single long flight
Purpose: Primary descent to Level 3B

A wide stone staircase descending at a steep angle from Level 2 to Level 3B. The walls are carved with funerary imagery --- priests carrying sheaves of grain and amphorae of oil, descending into the earth. The stairs are in good condition but the banisters have collapsed. The staircase takes its name from the bones that litter every third or fourth step --- the remains of temple servants and priests who died during the temple's fall, their skeletons tumbled down the stairs by decades of tremors.

Features:

  • Exits: North to Room 22 (Silo Floor Access); down to Level 3B
  • Illumination: Dark
  • Bones: Mostly harmless (old, brittle, no undead). However, at the midpoint of the staircase (20' down), 4 Skeletons and 1 Wight lie among the bones, animating and attacking when living creatures pass. The wight is the remains of a temple guard captain who died defending this stair during the temple's fall.
  • The Descent: 40 feet of steep stairs. A character who runs or fights on the stairs must make a DEX check each round or slip (fall 1d6 x 5 feet, taking 1d6 damage per 10 feet fallen).
  • Cold Air: The temperature drops noticeably on the staircase. By the bottom, breath is visible. The deeper levels are unnaturally cold --- the demon's influence.

DM Notes

Faction Dynamics on Level 2

Hekatos's Reaction to the Party: Hekatos is cautious but pragmatic. He would rather hire the party than fight them. His priorities are:

  1. Rescue Sarikos (if still captive)
  2. Retrieve Theron's spellbook from the silos
  3. Continue his research on the ward-stones (will not stop unless convinced of the demon threat)
  4. Avoid conflict with anyone who doesn't interfere with his work

Illaktamus's Behaviour: The worm is a territorial predator. It does not pursue outside its territory (Rooms 18--22). If driven from the silos by fire, it retreats downward to Level 3B and dens in the lower caverns for 1d4 days before returning. This gives the party a window to search the silos.

The Ward-Stones: The ward-stone in Room 12 is the only one on this level. The other two are on Level 3A (Room 9, among the high priests' tombs) and Level 3B (Room 9, in the collapsed shrine). If the party is working with Galvikta, protecting all three is a priority. If Hekatos destroys any, the consequences escalate:

  • One stone destroyed (of three): Seal integrity drops to 66%. Undead encounters on all levels increase by +1 frequency. Strange sounds (demonic whispers, distant screaming) become audible throughout the temple.
  • Two stones destroyed (of three): Seal integrity drops to 33%. The demon's influence manifests physically --- walls crack, black ichor seeps from stone, temperature drops 20 degrees. Adrianna can now project her voice to Level 2 and attempt to Command weak-willed NPCs. The sealed trapdoor in Room 15 can now be pushed open (the secondary seal fails).
  • All three destroyed: The seal breaks entirely. The sealed doors on Level 4 can be pushed open. See Factions.md for the catastrophic timeline.
